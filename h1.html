Computer Hardware: An Overview

Your guide to understanding the physical components of a computer system.

Input Devices

Access MINDMAP

Input devices allow users to interact with the computer and input data. Common examples include:

  • Keyboard: For typing text and commands.
  • Mouse: For pointing, selecting, and interacting with graphical interfaces.
  • Scanner: Converts physical documents into digital formats.
  • Microphone: Captures sound for recording or voice commands.

Output Devices

Output devices display or produce the results of computer processing. Examples include:

  • Monitor: Displays visual output, such as text, images, and videos.
  • Printer: Produces a physical copy of digital documents or images.
  • Speakers: Output audio signals for music, videos, or alerts.

Storage Devices

Storage devices retain data and programs for immediate or future use. These include:

  • Hard Disk Drives (HDD): Magnetic storage for large amounts of data.
  • Solid-State Drives (SSD): Faster, flash-based storage.
  • USB Drives: Portable storage devices for easy data transfer.
  • Optical Discs: CDs, DVDs, and Blu-ray discs for media or data storage.

Processing Unit

The Central Processing Unit (CPU) is the "brain" of the computer, executing instructions and managing tasks. It consists of:

  • Control Unit (CU): Directs data flow and execution.
  • Arithmetic Logic Unit (ALU): Performs mathematical and logical operations.
  • Cache Memory: Stores frequently accessed data for faster processing.

Motherboard

The motherboard is the main circuit board connecting all components, including the CPU, RAM, and storage. It also houses the chipset, which controls communication between the CPU and other hardware.

Power Supply Unit (PSU)

The PSU converts electricity from a wall outlet into a usable form for the computer's components.
